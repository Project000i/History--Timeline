Kyrgyz Influencers

Barsbek-Kagan

Barsbek-Kagan - ruler of the Yenisei Kyrgyz in the Minusinsk Basin in the 7th century AD.
Year and place of birth: 637, Tsigu state
Year and place of death:711, the battle in the forest of Sunga. In 711 Barsbek-Kagan died with other Kyrgyz warriors defending his people in a battle in the forest of Sunga against the ruler of the Second Turkic Kaganate.Barsbek-Kagan is an important person in the history of the Kyrgyz because this information about him tells us that in the Middle Ages the Kyrgyz had their own state.In conclusion, Barsbek-Kagan was a good ruler of the Kyrgyz because he thought about his people and was willing to die in battle to protect his people.

Jusup Balasagyn

Yusuf Hass Hajib Balasaguni was a Turkic poet and thinker who lived in the 11th century in the Karakhanid state, in the cities of Kashgar and Balasagun.
Year and place of birth: 1016-1017 in the town of Balasagun (near the modern city of Tokmok, Kyrgyzstan).
Year and place of death: 1075 in the city of Kashgar (Modern China). Interesting fact: In 2000 the image of Balasaguni appeared on the front side of the banknote of 1,000 soms, and in 2024 this banknote was updated. The portrait was made with the help of hand engraving.In 2016, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Azerbaijan and Turkey celebrated the thousandth anniversary of the birth of the outstanding thinker and poet. That year the international organisation TURKSOY declared Balasaguni. Kyrgyz politicians and historians extolled Balasaguni as the father of Kyrgyz and Islamic-Turkic literature and science, and his poem "Kutadgu Bilig" ("Gracious Knowledge") was presented as a masterpiece and a reflection of the period of revival of Turkic and East Islamic cultures.

Muhammed-Kyrgyz

Muhammed-Kyrgyz or Tagai-biy - one of the founders of Kyrgyz statehood, who lived in the 15th century A.D. Muhammed-Kyrgyz is known for his ability to rally and unite the Kyrgyz in the struggle against the Mogolistan invaders.
Year and place of birth: About 1470 in the pasture of Sary-bel (Alai valley, modern Kyrgyzstan).
Year and place of death: In captivity in the area of Kashgar (Modern China) in 1533. He finally formed the ethno-political unification of the Kyrgyz: the Right Wing, the Left Wing, and the Inner Link. Muhammed-Kyrgyz is important for the history of the Kyrgyz because he united Kyrgyz clans and tribes, and even after his death the Kyrgyz were united and were ready to do anything for each other.
